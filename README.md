## Understanding Kratom Capsule Types and Formats

### Gelatin vs. Vegetarian Capsules

Most kratom capsules use either gelatin or vegetarian (HPMC) capsule shells. The distinction matters for buyers with dietary restrictions — gelatin capsules are derived from animal sources while HPMC capsules are plant-based.

Quality vendors specify the capsule shell type on every product page. Brands that do not disclose capsule shell composition are omitting information that matters to a significant portion of buyers.

### Standard Powder Capsules vs. Extract Capsules

Standard kratom capsules contain ground leaf powder — typically 500 to 700 milligrams per capsule depending on capsule size. A standard serving of 2 to 4 capsules delivers 1 to 2.8 grams of powder.

Extract capsules contain concentrated kratom extract rather than raw powder. Per-capsule mitragynine content is significantly higher. Serving sizes are smaller — often 1 to 2 capsules. The precision requirements for extract capsule production are correspondingly greater, which is why extract capsule brands must demonstrate finished product testing.

Research from the University of Florida (2021) confirmed that extraction processes affect alkaloid concentration and secondary compound ratios. (Source: Journal of Natural Products, University of Florida, 2021) Finished product testing of extract capsules specifically confirms that concentration levels match label claims after the extraction and encapsulation process.

### Capsule Size and Fill Weight Standards

Standard kratom capsule sizes in descending fill capacity: 000 (approximately 800 to 1000mg), 00 (approximately 500 to 700mg), 0 (approximately 400 to 500mg). Most kratom capsule products use 00 size as the standard.

Fill weight consistency matters for dosing accuracy. Quality brands calibrate automated filling equipment to within 5% of target fill weight across the entire batch. Brands without proper equipment or quality controls produce capsules that vary by 15 to 25% within the same batch — effectively making per-capsule dosing claims meaningless.

Powder offers maximum flexibility. Serving sizes are fully adjustable in real time. Cost per milligram of mitragynine is generally lower than encapsulated formats. Onset typically occurs within 15 to 30 minutes.

### Capsules vs. Liquid Extracts

Liquid extracts deliver the fastest onset — typically 10 to 20 minutes. They carry the highest alkaloid concentration per serving and the highest quality risk if sourced from an unverified vendor.

Capsules are the more measured option. Lower concentration per serving. Consistent dosing. Appropriate for regular daily use by a wide range of experience levels. For buyers stepping up from powder who want more convenience without the intensity of a concentrated liquid extract, capsules are the logical intermediate format.

### Capsules vs. Gummies

Both formats offer similar convenience advantages over powder. Capsules have a longer shelf life than gummies. They are less sensitive to moisture, temperature, and light exposure. Cost per serving is generally lower for capsules than for gummy formats.

Gummies offer a more palatable consumption experience. Capsules offer better shelf stability and more widespread strain variety. Both formats require the same core quality standards — per-unit dosing disclosure and finished product testing.
